Graph vs. Relational Dynamics

Goal: Shift from analyzing isolated data points to analyzing the connections between them.

  • Relational Limitation: Joins are computed at query-time, which becomes exponentially slower as data scales.
  • Graph Advantage: Relationships are first-class citizens, physically stored as direct pointers.
  • Traversal: Connections are traversed via pointers rather than being "calculated".
Taxonomy & Paths
  • Graph: A system describing entities and their relations or interactions.
  • Network: A real-world dataset containing entities and their relationships.
  • Eulerian Path: A path that traverses every edge in the graph exactly once.
Scenario Identification

Self-Referencing: Occurs when a node's edge points back to itself, such as in guidance loops or social media self-interactions.

Optimal Routing: Critical for logistics, infrastructure management, and networking contact paths.

Hidden Relations: Modeled as user-item networks to examine indirect patterns in voting or purchasing.

Machine Learning Metrics
  • Node Degree: The count of a node's relationships. High-degree nodes represent major transmission lines, while low-degree nodes represent residential transformers.
  • Betweenness Centrality: Identifies influence over information flow.
  • Bottlenecks: A node (like a bridge) may have low degree but high centrality, acting as a sole bottleneck for traffic.
