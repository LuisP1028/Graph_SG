METAGRAPH & SOURCE LINKING MODULE_V1.0
METAGRAPH ARCHITECTURE

Goal: Multi-level abstraction of complex systems where nodes represent entire subgraphs.

  • Key 01: A metagraph is a generalized graph where edges connect sets of source nodes to sets of target nodes.
  • Key 02: It also describes a hierarchical structure where nodes represent entire subgraphs.
EXTRACTION & SOURCE LINKING

Goal: Maintain provenance (the record of where a specific node or edge came from, who created it, and how it has been modified over time) and avoid "Frankenstein" nodes during initial extraction.

  • Atomic Nodes: Every mention of "Person A" on chunk 1 is a distinct node from "Person A" on chunk 2.
  • Disambiguation: If Page 5 mentions "Jordan" (the CEO) and Page 80 mentions "Jordan" (the intern), merging them immediately creates a Frankenstein node with conflicting data.
  • Provenance & Explainability: In RAG or GraphRAG, when a model claims "Person X did Y," you can trace the exact chunk that provided that fact for verification.
NORMALIZATION AND CLEANSING MODULE_V1.1
STANDARDIZATION & REFINEMENT

Goal: Ensure graph connectivity by merging variations of the same concept.

  • Standardization: Lowercasing "Engineer" and "engineer" treats them as a single Occupation node to prevent fragmentation.
  • Cleansing: Task-specific process of refining raw mentions to match your domain's logic, such as stripping "Dr." or "CEO".
GRAPH-BASED ENTITY RESOLUTION MODULE_V1.2

Determines if two nodes represent the same real-world entity by analyzing their connections rather than just their names.

RELATIONAL CONTEXT & LOUVAIN

Goal: Use structural evidence and global interaction density to resolve entities.

  • Context is connectivity: If two candidate nodes share common neighbors (LIVES_IN, ATTENDED, MENTIONED), the structural evidence for merging them grows.
  • Louvain Algorithm: Analyzes global interaction density to identify neighborhoods where nodes are more tightly connected to each other than to the rest of the graph.
  • Mathematical Evidence: If "John Doe" from a maritime document and "John Doe" from astrophysics are placed into different communities, they belong to separate spheres.
HOPS & TRAVERSAL MODULE_V1.3
ECOSYSTEM MAPPING

Goal: Identify influencers while avoiding query explosion.

  • 2-Hops: Used to see the ecosystem around a target and identify "hidden hands" or influencers.
  • Avoid 3+ Hops: In dense graphs, this leads to query explosion where everything becomes connected, rendering data meaningless.
RETRIEVING HISTORY OF A TOPIC MODULE_V1.4
AGGLOMERATIVE HIERARCHICAL CLUSTERING

Goal: Capture the entire history of a topic across different levels of granularity via a Taxonomic Overlay.

  • Embedding Generation: Create high-dimensional vectors for every entity (Occupations, Concepts, Materials) using models like SentenceBERT.
  • AHC (Bottom-Up): Recursively merges the most similar clusters based on embedding distance.
  • The Dendrogram: Creates a tree structure where specific entities (Heavy Nitrogen) are leaf nodes merging into broader clusters (Isotopes) and domain-level categories (Nuclear Physics).
  • Upward Generalization: Querying "Nuclear Physics" automatically includes all facts related to its children (Isotopes, Cyclotrons).
